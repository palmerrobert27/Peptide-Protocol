12-Week Progression Model

Weeks 1–3 · Foundation

Learn the movements. Log every weight. Aim for RPE targets. Cardio at the lower end of prescribed intensities. Build the base.

Weeks 4–6 · Load

Add weight to main lifts weekly. Reduce rest periods by 15 sec on accessory work. Cardio intensity increases by 1 level.

Weeks 7–9 · Intensify

Drop sets on all isolation movements. Add 1 extra ab exercise per session. Cardio duration extends by 5 min.

Weeks 10–11 · Peak

Max effort on all main lifts. Minimal rest between supersets. All cardio at upper intensity range.

Week 12 · Deload + Test

50% volume. Test strength benchmarks. Take measurements. Plan the next block from your new baseline.

Nutrition

300–500 calorie deficit daily. 242g+ protein minimum — non-negotiable for muscle retention while cutting. Carbs timed around training, fats in other meals.

Abs Strategy

Abs are revealed by fat loss and built by weighted training. The cable crunch and hanging leg raise are your two most important movements — progress weight on cable crunch like any other lift.

Chest Priority

Sunday is your primary chest day — heaviest pressing happens here. Saturday provides a second stimulus via antagonist supersets. Two training doses per week accelerates development significantly.

Rest Days — Tue / Thu

Full rest or light activity only. A 20-min walk, gentle mobility work, or nothing at all. Recovery is where adaptation happens — don't compress it.

Flexibility

Over 12 weeks of daily hip openers, thoracic rotations, and targeted post-session stretching, your range of motion will transform significantly. The warmup work is not optional.

Tracking

Log lifts weekly. Progress photos every 2 weeks. Scale weight is unreliable — use measurements and how your clothes fit as primary indicators.
